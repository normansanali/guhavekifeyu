2022, May

Alwyn explained why he chose the name William Bowery for his songwriting credits on Folklore and Evermore during an appearance on The Kelly Clarkson Show.

“It was a combination of William, my great-grandfather [who] was a composer [who] I actually never met,” he explained. “He composed a lot of classical music and a lot of film scores.” Then there’s the Bowery, which is a neighborhood in New York where I spent a lot of time when I first moved there. So, band them together.”

The actor went on to say that he and his girlfriend decided to keep his identity hidden at first so that fans would approach the songs with no preconceived notions about his involvement. “We chose to do it so that people would first listen to the music before dissecting the fact that we did it together,”

he explained. “[The name] sounds like an Agatha Christie character, complete with a monocle and a big mustache.”
