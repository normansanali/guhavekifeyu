Maureen Pugh’s Career

Pugh was chosen as a member of the Westland District Council in 1998 and held that position for two years until becoming the district’s first female mayor, replacing John Drylie, in 2004.

She ran uncontested for mayor again in 2007.

[4] She resigned during the 2013 elections, and Michael Havill took over as mayor.

Pugh was charged with failing to disclose a financial conflict of interest while serving as mayor of the Westland District Council.

Pugh ran for the New Zealand National Party in the West Coast-Tasman constituency in the 2014 general election.

She narrowly lost out on a list position despite being rated 52 on the National Party list. She would have been elected to Congress based on preliminary results, but in the final tally, National’s share of the party vote fell, giving Steffan Browning of the Green Party the seat she would have held.

After Tim Groser’s resignation from the National Party in December 2015, Pugh continued to be first in line should a vacancy occur in a list seat held by a National Party MP during the 51st New Zealand Parliament. On February 9, 2016, following the summer recess, she was sworn in as a member of parliament.
